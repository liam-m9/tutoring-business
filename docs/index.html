About

I'm John. I'm in my eighth year of teaching and tutoring maths. Before that I spent a long time in broadcast journalism — teaching came later, because I wanted to help young people reach their potential.

I chose maths because I noticed the same students kept getting told they were "bad at maths" when really they just needed a handful of key concepts taught properly. Once those click, the rest follows.

Why a group, not 1:1

  • Everyone's in the same boat. No student feels like the only one who needs help.
  • No over-reliance on hand-holding, which tends to hurt independent learning.
  • Peer learning genuinely speeds up progress.
  • 1:1 can be intense and, for some students, more stressful than useful.
  • About a third of the cost of private 1:1 tutoring at the same intensity.
